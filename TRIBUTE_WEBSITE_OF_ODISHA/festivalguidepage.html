Explore Festivals With Us

Odisha, a state on the eastern coast of India, is known for its rich cultural heritage and vibrant festivals. Odisha celebrates 13 festivals in 12 months as the saying goes Bāra Māsare Tera Parba. These festivals are an integral part of the state's tradition, reflecting its religious fervor, social customs, and the harmonious blending of different cultures.

Makar Sankranti

During the festival, the people of Odisha offer prayers and food to the Sun God and seek blessings for a healthy and prosperous life. It holds astronomical importance for those who believe that life is affected by the movement of celestial bodies.

Dola Purnima

Dola Purnima marks the end of six-day swing festival of Radha and Lord Krishna. The Dola Jatra begins from the Falguna Dasami day. The idol of village deities, especially of Lord Krishna, is carried to all the homes in the villages on a decorated Veemana (palanquin). The people carrying the idols and those following it are semared with Abira.

Kalinga Mahotsav, Bhubaneswar

Kalinga Mahotsav or National Festival of Martial Dance is celebrated to mark the major victory of peace over war when Kalinga (part of Odisha) once witnessed a battle during the reign of Ashoka. The festival is a tribute to the martyrs of the Maurya Dynasty which is celebrated by various Martial Art Acts through dance and music.

Sital Sasthi, Sambalpur

Sital Sasthi celebrates the marriage of Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati. The festival is celebrated on the sixth day of the bright fortnight in the Hindu month of Jyestha which corresponds to the months of May-June in the Gregorian calendar. The three-day celebration is called the Sital Sasthi Yatra, wherein a family is chosen to play the role of Goddess Parvati's family to propose marriage to Lord Shiva. Famous Festivals in Odisha

Pana Sankranti, Bhadrak

Pana Sankranti is also known as Mahavishuva Sankranti. This festival marks the Odia New Year and is celebrated with traditional rituals, including the preparation and sharing of "pana," a special drink made from fruits, water, and other ingredients. The festival is characterized by various cultural events, processions, and community gatherings.[…]

Raja Parba

Raja Parba is a three-day long festival dedicated to Basu- Mata , the Earth Goddess. It is believed that during this period the Goddess undergoes her menstrual cycle, and to respect the womanhood of the Mother Earth, all the activities such as ploughing, plucking, cutting trees, that can hurt nature are prevented.

Rath-Yatra, Puri

Ratha Yatra is a major Hindu festival associated with Lord Jagannath held at Puri in India during the months of June or July. The 3 deities, Lord Jagannath, his elder brother Lord Balabhadra and their sister Subhadra are worshiped within the temple, on this festival they are taken to the streets of Puri so that everyone can have the fortune to see them. The 3 deities make an annual journey to their aunt's Temple(Gundicha Temple).

Kumar Purnima

Kumar Purnima occurs on the first full moon day of the month of Ashvin. It is primarily celebrated by unmarried girls who pray for a handsome husband. According to the belief, the handsome god Kartikeya also known as Kumar was born on this day. Girls also play traditional games like puchi on this day.

Also known as Gandhalepana Yatra, The 42-day long festival is celebrated by worshiping the deities with sandal mixed water. The deities are taken out of the temple for a holy patron in water in the traditional boats called ‘ Chapa’.

Durga Puja

Durga Puja, a popular festival, is celebrated to worship the Goddess of Power and War, the Goddess Durga and celebrate Naari-shakti (women power). The festival takes place in the month of Aswin or Kartik i.e. September or October every year according to the English calendar.

Frequently asked Questions....
What is the most famous festival of Odisha?
Rath Yatra in Puri is the most famous festival of Odisha, attracting millions of devotees from around the world.
What is the importance of Panchuka in Odisha?
Panchuka is observed during the last five days of the holy month of Kartika. Devotees refrain from eating non-vegetarian food and engage in religious activities, concluding with Boita Bandana on Kartika Purnima.
What is the significance of the Ratha Yatra at Baripada?
The Ratha Yatra at Baripada is significant because it allows women to pull the chariot of Subhadra, which is a unique tradition compared to other Ratha Yatras.
Where and when is the Konark Festival held?
The Konark Festival is held every year in December at the Sun Temple in Konark, featuring classical dance performances from various Indian dance forms.
How is Durga Puja celebrated in Cuttack?
Durga Puja in Cuttack is celebrated with great enthusiasm, featuring beautifully crafted silver and gold medhas (backdrops) and elaborate pandals.
What is Chhau and where is it predominantly celebrated in Odisha?
Chhau is a traditional dance form that incorporates martial arts, mock combat, and folk theatre. It is predominantly celebrated in Baripada, Mayurbhanj district.
